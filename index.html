About SavannahIQ

We read
the land

SavannahIQ is a land intelligence platform built for Africa — turning raw satellite data into decisions for farmers, governments and the organisations that serve them.

The problem

Decisions made in the dark

Africa's governments, food agencies and agricultural organisations are making decisions worth hundreds of billions of dollars based on data that is coarse, delayed and incomplete. Drought declarations come weeks after crops fail. Aid arrives after hunger sets in.

At the farm level it is worse. By the time a smallholder farmer knows something is wrong, the window to act has already closed. The land was sending signals weeks earlier — but nobody was reading them.

Agricultural insurers face the same blindness from a different angle. A farmer files a drought claim months after the event and there is no independent record of what actually happened on that land during that period. Insurers either pay out on faith or reject claims they cannot verify. Both erode the business — and neither serves the farmer.

What we built

Intelligence at every scale

SavannahIQ processes Sentinel-2 satellite data at 10-metre resolution — updated every 30 days — and delivers it as six actionable intelligence layers: vegetation health, land cover, drought score, rainfall, soil moisture and crop classification.

For organisations, this means early warning at county and sub-county level before a crisis. For farmers, this means knowing the health of their specific plot, how it has changed season by season, and what to do next. For insurers, every registered farm has a continuous satellite record — when a claim is filed, the data for that specific polygon during that specific period already exists.

The pilot

Bungoma first

We began in Bungoma County, Western Kenya — digitising 38,360 individual farm boundaries against high-resolution satellite imagery. Every farm boundary is now stored and ready for farm-level intelligence delivery through the Shamba Portal.

Bungoma is the proof of concept. The methodology, pipeline and intelligence framework is built to replicate across every county in Kenya and ultimately across the continent.

The vision

One country at a time. All of Africa.

Kenya is live. Ethiopia, Tanzania and Uganda are next. The goal is a continent-wide nervous system for land — where any farmer, government or organisation can access satellite intelligence about any piece of land in Africa, updated continuously.
