The problem

Decisions made in the dark

Africa's governments, international food agencies and agricultural organisations are making decisions worth hundreds of billions of dollars based on data that is coarse, delayed and incomplete.

Drought declarations come weeks after crops fail. Aid arrives after hunger sets in. Climate reports describe what happened years ago. The land is changing faster than any institution can track it.

"By the time a drought is officially declared, the window for early intervention has already closed."

SavannahIQ is built to close that gap — delivering 10-metre satellite intelligence, updated every 15 days, as actionable data not raw outputs.

Day 0
Drought stress begins at pixel level — detectable in NDVI and soil moisture
Day 15 — SavannahIQ detects
County drought score rises. NDVI declining. Soil moisture below threshold. Alert triggered.
Day 45
Visible crop stress. Farmers begin reporting losses. Local media picks up.
Day 90
Government commissions assessment. Field teams deployed.
Day 120+
Drought officially declared. Emergency relief coordinated. Crops already failed.
With SavannahIQ
Early warning at Day 15 enables pre-positioning of resources, advisory to farmers, policy response — before the crisis.
Intelligence layers

Six lenses on the land

Every layer is computed from best-available public satellite data and delivered as classified, actionable intelligence — not raw imagery.

LULC
Land Cover
9-class Dynamic World classification at 10m. Water, trees, grass, crops, shrub, built, bare — updated every satellite pass.
Source: Google Dynamic World V1 · Sentinel-2
COMPOSITE
Drought Score
Composite index combining NDVI, soil moisture, rainfall anomaly and LST. Scored 0–100. IPC-aligned classification.
Source: NDVI + SMAP + CHIRPS + MODIS
VEG
NDVI
Vegetation health index from Sentinel-2 NIR and red bands. Computed over vegetation land classes only. 15-day update window — detects crop stress, recovery and land change within a single growing period.
Source: Copernicus S2_SR_HARMONIZED
PRECIP
Rainfall
45-day actual rainfall in mm from CHIRPS daily precipitation model. Full Kenya coverage with no cloud gaps.
Source: UCSB-CHG CHIRPS Daily
HYDRO
Soil Moisture
Root-zone soil moisture from NASA SMAP microwave satellite. Penetrates cloud cover. Critical for crop stress detection.
Source: NASA SMAP SPL3SMP_E
TEMP
LST Anomaly
Land surface temperature deviation from 10-year baseline. Identifies heat stress zones and cooling from rainfall recovery.
Source: MODIS MOD11A2 · 8-day composite
