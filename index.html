The problem

Decisions made in the dark

Africa's governments, international food agencies and agricultural organisations are making decisions worth hundreds of billions of dollars based on data that is coarse, delayed and incomplete.

Drought declarations come weeks after crops fail. Aid arrives after hunger sets in. Climate reports describe what happened years ago. The land is changing faster than any institution can track it.

"By the time a drought is officially declared, the window for early intervention has already closed."

SavannahIQ is built to close that gap — delivering 10-metre satellite intelligence, updated every 30 days, as actionable data not raw outputs.

Intelligence layers

Six lenses on the land

Every layer is computed from best-available public satellite data and delivered as classified, actionable intelligence — not raw imagery.

LULC
Land Cover
9-class Dynamic World classification at 10m. Water, trees, grass, crops, shrub, built, bare — updated every satellite pass.
Source: Google Dynamic World V1 · Sentinel-2
COMPOSITE
Drought Score
Composite index combining NDVI, soil moisture and rainfall anomaly — weighted by agronomic importance. Scored 0–100, IPC-aligned. The synthesis that turns six signals into one decision.
Source: NDVI + SMAP + GPM IMERG
VEG
NDVI
Vegetation health index from Sentinel-2 NIR and red bands. Computed over vegetation land classes only. 30-day update window — detects crop stress, recovery and land change within a single growing period.
Source: Copernicus S2_SR_HARMONIZED
PRECIP
Rainfall
30-day actual rainfall in mm from GPM IMERG daily precipitation model. Full Kenya coverage with no cloud gaps — updated with every snapshot.
Source: NASA GPM IMERG V07
HYDRO
Soil Moisture
Root-zone soil moisture from NASA SMAP microwave satellite. Penetrates cloud cover. Critical for crop stress detection.
Source: NASA SMAP SPL3SMP_E
CLASS
Crop Classification
8-class crop type estimation from NDVI within Dynamic World cropland pixels — fallow, dryland cereals, maize stages, sugarcane, tea/coffee. Identifies what's at stake, not just that stress exists.
Source: Sentinel-2 NDVI · Dynamic World class 4 · Estimated

What makes us different

Every institution sees the problem.
SavannahIQ sees the pixel.

Every major food security institution — NDMA, FAO, FEWS NET, WFP — is looking at the same problem from 30,000 feet. They produce reports. SavannahIQ produces decisions.

01 · Resolution
10m vs 250m

FEWS NET, NDMA and WFP rely on MODIS at 250m–1km. At 1km one pixel is 100 hectares — an entire village's farmland. SavannahIQ processes Sentinel-2 at 10m through Dynamic World — each pixel is 100 square metres, roughly one farm plot. A stressed field inside a healthy landscape is invisible at 250m. At 10m it is a red patch with a precise location.

02 · Automation
30 days, automatically

NDMA bulletins are nominally monthly but depend on field reports compiled by hand — coverage gaps and delays are routine. FEWS NET outlooks span 4 to 6 weeks at 250m resolution. SavannahIQ updates every 30 days entirely from satellite data — no field teams, no manual compilation — at 10m resolution, with every index synthesised into one drought score and AI-generated guidance the moment the data lands.

03 · Precision
Ward vs county

Existing tools say "Garissa is at risk." SavannahIQ lets you zoom into Garissa and see that stress is concentrated in Dadaab sub-county, not Ijara — 68% of cropland pixels critical in one ward, 71% stable in the next. That is the difference between sending relief to a county headquarters and sending an extension officer to the exact location that needs help.

04 · Intelligence
Composite vs single index

NDVI alone does not tell you a crop is failing. Rainfall alone does not tell you a crop is stressed — it may have rained last week but soil moisture depleted overnight. SavannahIQ combines NDVI, rainfall anomaly and soil moisture into a single calibrated drought score — weighted by agronomic importance — so the composite reflects actual crop stress, not a single satellite signal in isolation.

05 · Masking
Farmland only

Every satellite index is contaminated when computed across an entire landscape — urban heat inflates readings in Nairobi, dense forest pushes NDVI high in Kakamega. SavannahIQ uses Dynamic World's land cover classification to compute drought intelligence only over agricultural pixels. The signal is clean because it only measures what matters for food security.

06 · Time series
Snapshots vs static maps

Land use change, crop growth stages, drought onset — none of these are visible in a snapshot. SavannahIQ.s 30-day snapshot archive lets a county officer watch Trans Nzoia's maize move through growth to harvest, or watch Baringo's rangeland NDVI collapse over six weeks during drought onset. That temporal dimension is what converts a map into intelligence.

SavannahIQ is the only platform in Africa that delivers sub-county, 30-day agricultural intelligence at 10m resolution — twenty-five times sharper than the 250m MODIS imagery used by leading food security institutions — masked to actual farmland, combining four satellite datasets into a composite drought score plus crop classification, accessible on any browser, built for the people who make food security decisions.
